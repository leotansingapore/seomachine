### 2. Wrote research brief — `content-workflow-management`

**File**: `research/brief-content-workflow-management-2026-07-08.md`  
**Primary keyword**: content workflow management (~300/mo, KD: ~28)  
**Secondary keywords**: content workflow, content production workflow, editorial workflow, content process management, content workflow template  
**Cluster**: Cluster 12 — Content Operations (Supporting #1)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. **No B2B SaaS-specific workflow framework** — competitor guides target enterprise media companies with 20-person teams; no guide addresses the 1–5-person B2B SaaS content team
2. **Workflow = tool setup** — most content is about setting up Asana or Trello boards, not designing the workflow itself; no guide draws the distinction between workflow design and workflow execution
3. **No handoff criteria coverage** — competitor content describes workflow stages but doesn't define what "done" looks like at each stage transition; articles still move informally
4. **Missing cycle time as a primary metric** — all competitor content focuses on output metrics; no guide treats cycle time as the primary workflow health indicator
5. **No scaling logic** — guides describe workflows for "small teams" or "large teams" without specifying what changes at each scale or what triggers the transition

**Differentiating angle**: The process design article — argues that most content quality and consistency problems are workflow problems, not writing problems. Delivers a 7-stage workflow framework with explicit handoff criteria, documentation templates, and scaling logic across solo / 3-person / 5–7-person team scales.
