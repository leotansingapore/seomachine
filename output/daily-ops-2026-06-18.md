### 2. Wrote research brief — `saas-pricing-page-optimization`

**File**: `research/brief-saas-pricing-page-optimization-2026-06-18.md`  
**Primary keyword**: SaaS pricing page optimization (~450/mo, KD: ~40)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 9 — Conversion Rate Optimization (Supporting #2)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. No pipeline-qualification framing — every competitor treats the pricing page as a decision page; for B2B SaaS it's actually a routing/qualification tool
2. No PLG vs. SLG architecture distinction — self-serve and sales-led pricing pages have fundamentally different design requirements
3. No multi-stakeholder breakdown — champion, CFO, IT, and procurement each read the pricing page for a different signal; no guide addresses all four
4. No ROI framing by tier — economic buyers don't evaluate features, they evaluate return; tier-level ROI anchors are the conversion element no competitor provides
5. No "Contact Sales" optimization — most-clicked enterprise CTA element, universally under-optimized; no competitor covers what to replace it with

**Differentiating angle**: A SaaS pricing page is a routing page, not a decision page. Its job is to send self-serve buyers through with no friction, give economic buyers the ROI frame they need to justify budget, and give enterprise prospects enough process transparency to submit the form. Design for routing first, conversion second.
