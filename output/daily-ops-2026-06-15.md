### 2. Wrote research brief — `trust-signals-for-b2b-saas`

**File**: `research/brief-trust-signals-for-b2b-saas-2026-06-15.md`  
**Primary keyword**: trust signals for B2B websites (~400/mo, KD: ~28)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 8 — E-E-A-T and Authority Building (Supporting #3)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. No three-layer trust signal taxonomy — every guide lists signals as a flat checklist without distinguishing technical/commercial/editorial layers or which evaluator each serves
2. No multi-stakeholder trust signal mapping — all guides assume a single buyer; no content maps signals to champion, CFO, IT/security lead, and procurement separately
3. No dual-ROI framing — trust signals are treated as CRO tools only; no competitor connects them to E-E-A-T and ranking signals in commercial SERPs
4. No editorial trust signals coverage — citations, author attribution, update date accuracy, and methodology transparency are completely absent from competitor guides
5. No trust signal audit framework — no competitor provides a structured assessment protocol for identifying gaps by layer and evaluator

**Differentiating angle**: Trust signals for B2B SaaS are a three-layer architecture, not a checklist. Each layer (technical, commercial, editorial) serves different evaluators (IT/Google, economic buyers/procurement, quality raters/champions) and delivers different outcomes (rankings, deal closure, content credibility). The dual-ROI case — same infrastructure improves both organic rankings and pipeline — is the business case that makes the investment defensible.
