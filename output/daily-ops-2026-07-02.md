### 2. Wrote research brief — `content-distribution-channels`

**File**: `research/brief-content-distribution-channels-2026-07-02.md`  
**Primary keyword**: content distribution channels (~500/mo, KD: ~40)  
**Secondary keywords**: b2b content distribution channels, content promotion channels  
**Cluster**: Cluster 11 — Content Distribution and Amplification (Supporting #1)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. **No channel selection framework** — all competitors list 15–25 channels without helping B2B teams decide which to prioritize based on audience fit, content format, and bandwidth constraints
2. **No B2B-specific benchmarks per channel** — no calibration for what "good" looks like on LinkedIn, email, or Reddit for B2B SaaS audiences (all existing benchmarks are generic marketing averages)
3. **No per-channel activation playbooks** — competitors describe channels conceptually; the gap between "use LinkedIn" and the specific moves (native post format, 60-minute comment seeding window, DM outreach sequence) is where most distribution fails
4. **No content-channel fit by format** — research reports, case studies, how-to guides, and opinion posts each have different natural distribution homes; no competitor maps format to highest-leverage channel
5. **No minimum viable distribution stack** — no competitor answers the question for 1–2 person content teams: "If I can only do 3 channels consistently, which 3 should they be?"

**Differentiating angle**: This is a channel selection guide, not a channel encyclopedia. Core argument: most B2B content teams should activate 3–4 channels and do them well, not spread thinly across 12. The article provides a channel selection matrix, per-channel activation playbooks for the 6 channels that matter for B2B SaaS, B2B benchmarks for each, and a minimum viable distribution stack for teams with < 5 hours/week.
