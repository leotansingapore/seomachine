### 2. Wrote research brief — `b2b-link-building-strategy`

**File**: `research/brief-b2b-link-building-strategy-2026-06-09.md`  
**Primary keyword**: B2B link building strategy (~900/mo, KD: Medium-Low ~42)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 7 — B2B SEO Strategy (Supporting Article #3)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. No account-based link building framework — applying ABM logic to publication targeting rather than mass outreach
2. No pipeline attribution model for B2B links — measuring which backlinks appear in the research journey of closed accounts, not just DA/volume
3. No analyst relations as a structured SEO channel — Gartner, Forrester, G2 coverage treated as PR, not as an engineered link program
4. No partner co-marketing link architecture — integration partner pages and joint content as systematic link acquisition
5. No customer advocacy link program — links from customer company blogs are uniquely high-trust in B2B but completely absent from standard guides

**Differentiating angle**: B2B link building is a relationship system, not an outreach campaign. The highest-value links (analyst reports, integration partners, customer company blogs, trade publications) are all relationship-gated. Account-based link targeting — identifying the 40-60 publications your ICP reads before buying, then building editorial relationships with those sources — is the framework. Pipeline attribution (surveying closed accounts to find which publications influenced their decision) is the measurement.

**Distinction from Cluster 4 (Link Building for B2B SaaS):**
- Cluster 4 pillar: Six highest-ROI link acquisition *tactics* for B2B SaaS (product-led magnets, guest posts, original data, integration pages). Execution focus.
- This article: Strategic *program architecture* for B2B link building — account-based targeting, five relationship channels, operational system, pipeline-aligned measurement. How to think about and run the program, not which tactics to execute within it.
