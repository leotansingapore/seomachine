### 2. Wrote research brief — `plg-onboarding`

**File**: `research/brief-plg-onboarding-2026-06-30.md`  
**Primary keyword**: PLG onboarding (~350/mo, KD: ~38)  
**Secondary keyword**: product-led growth onboarding strategy (~200/mo, KD: ~36)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 10 — SaaS Onboarding Optimization (Supporting #3)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. PLG onboarding treated as feature addition (checklists, tooltips) vs. architectural redesign — no competitor establishes that PLG onboarding is the product taking over the sales rep's job: value explanation, objection handling, friction detection, guidance to aha moment. Adding a checklist widget to a product designed for SLG doesn't create PLG onboarding.
2. No empty state design framework — empty states are the highest-leverage PLG onboarding touchpoint (first thing every new user sees) but are absent from all competitor guides. No guide covers the three empty state design approaches (sample data, pre-filled first action, single guided action) or what makes an empty state effective vs. a conversion killer.
3. No upgrade trigger taxonomy — three distinct models (usage-based, feature-gate, seat-limit) each require different UX design, copy approach, and email support. No competitor distinguishes them or provides design guidance for each.
4. No SLG-to-PLG migration framework — all competitor guides assume greenfield PLG builds; the majority of SaaS companies adding PLG are migrating from SLG with existing product architecture, sales motions, and customer data.
5. No PLG-specific metrics framework — PLG onboarding has fundamentally different metrics than SLG (activation rate vs. SQL rate, time-to-aha vs. time-to-close, PQL rate vs. MQL rate). No competitor provides PLG-specific metric definitions with benchmark ranges.

**Differentiating angle**: In SLG, the sales rep is the onboarding system. In PLG, the product is. PLG onboarding is the architectural design of how the product does what the sales rep used to do — not a feature set to be bolted on. The article provides the five in-product onboarding layers, the three upgrade trigger models with design guidance, a three-step PQL definition method, and the PLG-specific metrics framework.

### 3. Wrote full supporting article — `plg-onboarding` (Cluster 10 Supporting #3)

**Article summary:**
- **Primary keyword**: PLG onboarding (~350/mo, KD: ~38)
- **Cluster**: Cluster 10 — SaaS Onboarding Optimization (Supporting #3)
- **H1**: PLG onboarding: the self-serve activation architecture (not a feature checklist)

**Key sections delivered:**
- Intro hook: In SLG, the sales rep is the onboarding system. In PLG, the product is. Most teams add checklists on top of a product designed for SLG and wonder why activation rate didn't move.
- PLG vs. SLG onboarding — the design substitution: 6-job comparison (value explanation, objection handling, expectation setting, friction detection, aha moment guidance, expansion), comparison table across 6 dimensions; implication: you can't add PLG onboarding on top of an SLG product
- Five in-product onboarding layers: (1) empty state design — three approaches (sample data, pre-filled first action, single guided action); (2) minimum activation sequence — cohort analysis to find the shortest path to signal event; (3) contextual guidance — fires at friction moments, not at login; (4) progress indicators — minimum activation sequence only, tied to value not just action; (5) friction detection — product analytics + event-triggered outreach
- Upgrade trigger taxonomy — three models: usage-based (feel earned, not punitive; show progress to limit; lead with accomplishment), feature-gate (surface before moment of need; lock icon in navigation; lead with feature value), seat-limit (highest-intent trigger; frictionless upgrade path; fastest path to payment); design principle, copy approach, and email support for each
- PQL definition — three-step method: identify highest-converting users, find shared behavioral events, set threshold; PQL vs. signal event distinction; how PQL threshold feeds upgrade trigger timing
- PLG email layer as support architecture: email reaches users outside the session (when the product can't); stall intervention (earlier detection thresholds in PLG because product analytics show exact stuck point); activation nudges (highly specific because event log is rich); feature unlock emails (fire on activation event, not Day X); what email does not do (replace the product)
- PLG-specific metrics table: activation rate (benchmark: 20–40% freemium; 35–55% time-limited trial), time-to-aha, PQL rate, PQL-to-paid conversion, seat expansion rate, stall zone recovery rate; leading indicator chain (activation rate → PQL rate → conversion rate) as the diagnostic framework
- 5-question FAQ targeting PAA: what is PLG onboarding, how it differs from SLG, what tools are needed, what is a good activation rate, how to measure success
