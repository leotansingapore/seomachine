### 2. Wrote research brief — `saas-onboarding-emails`

**File**: `research/brief-saas-onboarding-emails-2026-06-29.md`  
**Primary keyword**: SaaS onboarding emails (~500/mo, KD: ~35)  
**Secondary keyword**: onboarding email sequence (~300/mo, KD: ~32)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 10 — SaaS Onboarding Optimization (Supporting #2)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. Time-based drip presented as best practice — all top-10 results teach Day 0 / Day 2 / Day 5 sequences that treat all users identically. No guide explains why time-based drip underperforms event-triggered design or what the behavioral alternative looks like.
2. No event trigger taxonomy — "event-triggered email" appears in advanced content but never with a concrete taxonomy of what events trigger what email types. No guide maps real SaaS product events (integration connected, project created, teammate invited) to specific email actions.
3. No email type taxonomy by onboarding stage — competitors conflate all onboarding emails as "the drip sequence." No guide distinguishes the five distinct types (welcome, activation nudge, stall intervention, feature unlock, trial expiry) with different jobs and different trigger conditions.
4. No stall zone re-engagement architecture — the stall zone (users who signed up, never activated, gone silent) is the highest-leverage intervention point. No competitor provides a specific 3-email stall re-engagement architecture with detection thresholds by trial length and a defined stop condition.
5. No subject line strategy mapped to email type — generic "subject line tips" without connecting them to the specific job each email type must accomplish. Subject line approach for a welcome email is fundamentally different from a stall intervention email.

**Differentiating angle**: Most SaaS teams send emails on a schedule to a list. They should be sending emails on trigger to a segment. The article provides the event-triggered email architecture: five email types with distinct jobs, trigger taxonomy with real product event examples, stall zone detection thresholds by trial length, and subject line strategy mapped to each type.
