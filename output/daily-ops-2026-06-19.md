### 2. Wrote research brief — `free-trial-to-paid-conversion-rate`

**File**: `research/brief-free-trial-to-paid-conversion-rate-2026-06-19.md`  
**Primary keyword**: free trial to paid conversion rate (~350/mo, KD: ~35)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 9 — Conversion Rate Optimization (Supporting #3)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. Treating conversion as a single event, not a 5-step sequence — every guide optimizes "the moment of conversion" without mapping activation → habit → limit → upgrade → payment as distinct stages with different drop-off drivers
2. No distinction between trial expiry conversion vs. limit-hit conversion — two different psychological moments requiring different email timing, copy framing, and upgrade prompt design
3. No upgrade prompt design guidance — the highest-leverage touchpoint in PLG SaaS is covered by no top-10 competitor; what to say, when to show it, how to handle the top objection inline
4. No benchmark breakdown by trial model — the "2–5%" standard conflates time-limited, feature-limited, and usage-limited trials; each has a different baseline
5. No event-driven email sequence architecture — every guide has a drip checklist, none gives the decision logic for which events trigger which emails

**Differentiating angle**: Free trial to paid is not one conversion event — it's a 5-step micro-conversion sequence. Most teams optimize steps 4 and 5 (upgrade consideration and payment) while the real drop-off is at steps 1 and 3 (activation and limit encounter). Fix the sequence in order.
