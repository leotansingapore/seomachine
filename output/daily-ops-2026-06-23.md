### 2. Wrote research brief — `user-activation-saas`

**File**: `research/brief-user-activation-saas-2026-06-23.md`  
**Primary keyword**: user activation SaaS (~400/mo, KD: ~35)  
**Secondary keyword**: SaaS activation rate (~200/mo, KD: ~30)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 10 — SaaS Onboarding Optimization (Supporting #1)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. Vanity activation metrics taught as correct practice — every guide treats checklist completion and login frequency as valid activation metrics, none distinguish between vanity metrics and signal events (actions that statistically predict 90-day retention).
2. No methodology for finding the signal event — every guide says "find your aha moment" without explaining how. The cohort analysis method (retained vs. churned cohort comparison, action verb filter, 30-day correlation test) is absent from all top-10 results.
3. No activation failure mode taxonomy — low activation rate is not one problem; it's five distinct failure modes (distance from aha moment, wrong aha moment, empty state, trigger timing, wrong channel mix). Competitors list generic tactics without diagnostic logic.
4. No benchmark data by product type or GTM motion — activation rate benchmarks vary significantly by PLG vs. SLG, product complexity, and ACV. No competitor provides segment-specific benchmarks.
5. No tactic playbook organized by failure mode — tactics are mixed without mapping to specific diagnoses, making it impossible to know which fix applies to which problem.

**Differentiating angle**: Most SaaS teams are measuring activation wrong. The signal event (the specific action that predicts 90-day retention) and the vanity metric (the action that looks like activation) are often different. This article provides the cohort analysis method to find the real signal event and the failure mode diagnostic framework to identify and fix what's actually broken.
