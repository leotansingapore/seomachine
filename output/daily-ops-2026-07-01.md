### 3. Wrote research brief — `b2b-content-distribution-strategy`

**File**: `research/brief-b2b-content-distribution-strategy-2026-07-01.md`  
**Primary keyword**: b2b content distribution strategy (~400/mo, KD: ~38)  
**Secondary keywords**: content distribution channels, content amplification strategy  
**Cluster**: Cluster 11 — Content Distribution and Amplification (PILLAR)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. No B2B-specific channel prioritization framework — all competitors list the same channels without weighting them for B2B SaaS audiences or providing a decision framework for channel selection
2. No distribution window model — the 72-hour post-publish period is the highest-leverage window for both engagement and SEO signals; no competitor covers it
3. No owned vs. earned vs. paid sequencing — the principle that you should activate owned first, then earned, then paid (only to amplify what's working) is covered conceptually but never as operational guidance
4. No content-channel fit matrix — what formats perform best on which channels; no competitor provides this as a planning tool for use before content is created
5. No distribution-to-SEO feedback loop — no competitor connects distribution effort (backlinks earned, behavioral signals seeded) to downstream ranking velocity

**Differentiating angle**: Most B2B content teams publish and announce to their existing audience. That's not distribution — it's announcement. The article establishes the owned → earned → paid sequence with the 72-hour window model and the distribution-to-SEO feedback loop that shows how distribution is an SEO mechanism, not a social media function.
