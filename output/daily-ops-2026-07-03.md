### 2. Wrote research brief — `content-repurposing-strategy`

**File**: `research/brief-content-repurposing-strategy-2026-07-03.md`  
**Primary keyword**: content repurposing strategy (~600/mo, KD: ~32)  
**Secondary keywords**: how to repurpose content, content repurposing for b2b, repurpose blog content  
**Cluster**: Cluster 11 — Content Distribution and Amplification (Supporting #2)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. **No repurposing selection filter** — all competitors assume every article is worth repurposing; the gap is a clear performance threshold for which content earns the investment
2. **No format-channel fit matrix** — competitors list formats and channels separately; the gap is a matrix showing which repurposing format maps to which channel and why
3. **No per-format time estimates** — teams need to plan bandwidth; no competitor quantifies how long each repurposing move takes
4. **No B2B-specific platform guidance** — all competitors treat LinkedIn, Twitter, Medium, and YouTube as equally viable; for B2B SaaS the channel hierarchy is specific
5. **No SEO feedback loop** — no competitor connects repurposing back to SEO outcomes (behavioral signals, backlink surface, branded search lift, Medium canonical equity)

**Differentiating angle**: This is a repurposing operations guide, not a format list. Core argument: repurposing is a distribution multiplier — one 2,500-word B2B guide, repurposed intelligently across 4 channels, reaches 5–10x more of the target audience than a second article on a different topic.
