### 3. Wrote research brief — `b2b-seo-strategy`

**File**: `research/brief-b2b-seo-strategy-2026-06-04.md`
**Primary keyword**: B2B SEO strategy (~4,200/mo, KD: Medium ~50)
**Cluster**: Cluster 7 — B2B SEO Strategy (Pillar)

**Six competitor gaps identified**:
1. **No pipeline connection** — every existing guide frames success as traffic/rankings, not organic MQLs or pipeline value
2. **No B2B funnel-stage keyword framework** — generic intent taxonomy applied to B2B misses the six-stage buyer journey (Problem Awareness → Solution Awareness → Category Definition → Provider Comparison → Validation → Procurement)
3. **No multi-stakeholder buyer coverage** — 5.4 stakeholders per average B2B tech purchase, each searching differently at each stage; zero guides map keywords to all stakeholders
4. **No long sales cycle content design** — 84-day average SaaS deal requires content architecture that supports buyers through the full journey, not just the first click
5. **The dark funnel problem** — 70% of B2B research happens anonymously before first sales contact; SEO is the only channel capturing this, but no guide frames B2B SEO as a dark funnel strategy
6. **No honest timeline** — every guide promises results without addressing that B2B SEO takes 12+ months to compound; unrealistic expectations kill programs before they succeed

**Differentiating angle**: "Pipeline-first content architecture" — frame B2B SEO as designing a system that captures buyers at every stage of a six-stage buyer journey, serving every stakeholder persona, with pipeline attribution as the success metric. Not a traffic strategy — a pipeline architecture.
