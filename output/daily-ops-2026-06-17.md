### 2. Wrote research brief — `saas-landing-page-best-practices`

**File**: `research/brief-saas-landing-page-best-practices-2026-06-17.md`  
**Primary keyword**: SaaS landing page best practices (~600/mo, KD: ~42)  
**Cluster**: Cluster 9 — Conversion Rate Optimization (Supporting #1)

**Five competitor gaps identified:**
1. No multi-evaluator architecture — every competitor designs for a single buyer; B2B buying committees have 6.8 stakeholders (Gartner, 2025) who review the page independently
2. No PLG vs. SLG distinction — free-trial and demo-request pages have fundamentally different conversion mechanics; no guide separates them cleanly
3. No intent-matched page architecture — landing page design doesn't account for the intent tier of incoming traffic (branded vs. category vs. informational visitors)
4. No specificity standards for social proof — guides say "add social proof" without specifying that B2B buyers need named customers, company-size context, and specific outcome metrics
5. No trust signal integration for gatekeeper evaluators — SOC 2, GDPR, SLA, and security documentation are conversion elements for IT/procurement, not decorative badges

**Differentiating angle**: B2B SaaS landing pages are reviewed by committees, not individuals. Structure each section for a different evaluator: above the fold for the champion (outcome + CTA), mid-page for the economic buyer (ROI anchors + named proof), lower page for gatekeepers (security/compliance signals).
