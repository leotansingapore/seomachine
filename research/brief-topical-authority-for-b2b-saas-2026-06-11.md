## Competitor Landscape — 5 Gaps

Existing SERP is dominated by Ahrefs, Semrush, SE Ranking, and Moz. All have the same structure: define topical authority → explain why it matters → publish more content → use internal links. None address B2B SaaS specifically. Five exploitable gaps:

**Gap 1: No buyer journey mapping**  
Every existing guide treats topical authority as an SEO-only concern. No competitor maps topical cluster architecture to buyer journey stages. For B2B SaaS — where the research phase spans 6–18 months and involves 5–8 stakeholders — this is the entire strategic point. We own this framing.

**Gap 2: No multi-stakeholder framework**  
B2B buying is multi-stakeholder. The champion (power user), the economic buyer (CFO/VP), the technical evaluator (IT/Security), and the blocker (procurement) each search differently. No topical authority guide addresses how to build coverage across all buyer personas, not just the primary champion.

**Gap 3: No revenue/pipeline connection**  
All existing guides measure topical authority by rankings, impressions, or "featured snippet coverage." None connect topical authority growth to pipeline metrics — influenced opportunities, content-assisted conversions, time-to-pipeline acceleration. This is the metric B2B SaaS leaders actually care about.

**Gap 4: No "authority decay" system**  
No guide addresses what happens when you've built topical authority and it starts eroding — competitive content, algorithm updates, industry terminology shifts, product category evolution. No competitor offers an authority maintenance system, just build instructions.

**Gap 5: No measurement framework beyond rankings**  
Branded search growth, cluster ranking coverage percentage, SERP presence rate, citation rate in buyer research — all legitimate topical authority proxies. None in existing content. We have the opportunity to introduce a 6-metric measurement framework.

---

## Differentiating Angle

**Core thesis**: Topical authority for B2B SaaS is not about publishing the most content on a topic. It's about being the most trusted, most complete information resource for every stakeholder involved in evaluating your category — across the entire buying journey.

This reframe shifts topical authority from an SEO output metric (rankings, impressions) to a buyer trust infrastructure that drives pipeline quality, deal velocity, and competitive differentiation. The mechanism: when all five stakeholders in a buying decision independently find your content authoritative, the sales conversation starts from a position of established trust.

### H2: What Topical Authority Actually Is (and Isn't)
- Not: the highest number of pages on a topic
- Not: keyword density across a cluster
- Is: search coverage completeness across the full topical map
- Is: depth-to-surface ratio (every page answers the full question, not part of it)
- Is: buyer journey coverage — content at every stage for every evaluator
- Is: recognized as the reference resource by Google and buyers alike
