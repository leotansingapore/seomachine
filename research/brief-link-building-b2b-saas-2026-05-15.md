## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Top Competitor Coverage Patterns
Most top-ranking articles cover:
- Generic link building tactics (guest posts, HARO, broken link building)
- Domain authority / DR metrics
- Outreach templates
- Basic directory submissions

### Content Gaps (What Competitors Miss)
1. **SaaS-specific link magnets**: No competitor explains how SaaS products themselves generate links (free tools, API documentation, integration pages, status pages)
2. **Integration ecosystem strategy**: The "get listed on partner app marketplaces" angle is almost entirely absent from competitor content
3. **Review site link velocity**: G2/Capterra/GetApp links are undervalued; competitors treat them as an afterthought rather than a structured acquisition channel
4. **Long sales cycle context**: B2B SaaS buyers consume content for months. Link building should build topical authority in the buyer's research journey, not just generate raw link count
5. **Link quality vs. volume for SaaS**: For B2B SaaS, three links from industry analysts beat 300 links from generic blogs. No competitor makes this distinction clearly.
6. **Data study methodology**: Original research is cited heavily in B2B SaaS, but no guide explains how to produce a data study on a lean budget

### Differentiation Strategy
Lead with the "why B2B SaaS is different" frame immediately. Generic link building advice optimizes for volume and DA. B2B SaaS optimizes for topical authority in a specific vertical where a single link from the right analyst or review aggregator can move more needle than 50 generic guest posts. Frame every tactic around: (1) is it scalable? (2) does it fit the SaaS buyer journey? (3) what's the realistic timeline?
