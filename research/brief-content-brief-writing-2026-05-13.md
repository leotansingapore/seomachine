## Search intent

Searchers are either:
1. **Content managers / SEO leads** who want a system for briefing writers -- they need a process and a template
2. **Freelance writers** who've been handed bad briefs and want to know what good looks like

Both audiences want actionable output: a template or a checklist they can use today. This is a "give me the thing" query, not a conceptual explainer. The article needs to deliver the template and the process within the first half.

---

## Competitor gap analysis

**Ahrefs / Semrush blog posts** on content briefs:
- Cover 7-10 components in a list format
- Heavy on "what to include" but light on "how to actually write it" (the process)
- Don't address the most common failure mode: briefs that are technically complete but that writers ignore because they're formatted as data dumps

**Our differentiation**:
1. Lead with the failure mode (ignored briefs) -- most guides assume the brief will be read
2. Writer psychology: briefs get ignored when they look like spreadsheets and don't explain the "why" behind each instruction
3. Add a "writer's perspective" section -- brief from both the content manager and writer POV
4. Include a real-world example section (redacted client brief) -- competitors use generic examples
5. Connect brief quality to output quality with a testable claim: a brief that takes 45 mins to write saves 2-3 hours of revision
