## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Why vendors dominate this SERP
Ahrefs, Semrush, and Moz all rank for "keyword difficulty" because they define it differently -- their articles explain their own metric. This creates a gap: no neutral third-party content explains when to trust ANY of these scores, how they differ, or when KD is misleading.

### Content Gaps (Our Opportunity)
1. **No "when to ignore KD" content**: Every competitor article tells you what KD is. None tells you the four situations where KD is actively misleading and you should override it.
2. **No cross-tool comparison**: Tools give wildly different KD scores for the same keyword. Ahrefs, Semrush, and Moz don't agree. Nobody explains why or what to do about it.
3. **New site context missing**: KD advice is written for established domains. New sites need a different KD threshold framework -- nobody covers this.
4. **No integration with content planning**: KD is treated as a standalone metric, not a decision input in a content prioritization system.

### Differentiation Strategy
Lead with the contrarian angle: "KD is an estimate, not a verdict." Most marketers treat KD as a gate (above 60 = don't bother). We reframe it as one input in a multi-factor decision. Provide the override conditions and a practical target-setting framework by domain authority tier.

## 4. Supporting Elements

### Statistics to Include
1. Ahrefs KD and Semrush KD for the same keyword can differ by 20-30 points (industry observation -- note as "tool-dependent")
2. 70% of all searches are long-tail keywords (Ahrefs) -- most have KD under 30
3. 96.55% of pages get zero organic traffic (Ahrefs) -- low-KD keyword targeting is the primary lever for new sites
4. Domain authority is the strongest predictor of ranking ability for high-KD terms -- but content quality can override it for KD 40-65 range

### Mini-Stories
1. **Introduction**: James at an SEO agency -- client in HR tech, target keyword KD 72, advises skip. Competitor with DA 35 (lower than client's DA 42) ranks #3 because SERP has three thin 600-word posts. Gap analysis would have caught it. KD alone didn't.
