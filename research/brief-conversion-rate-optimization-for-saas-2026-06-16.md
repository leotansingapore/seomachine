## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Top Competitor Approaches
1. **Generic CRO platforms (Hotjar, Unbounce, CXL)**: Cover CRO broadly — heatmaps, A/B tests, landing page copy. Not B2B SaaS-specific. Assume paid traffic. No buying committee coverage.

2. **SaaS-adjacent content (HubSpot, Intercom, Appcues)**: Focus on product onboarding and in-app CRO. No SEO-to-conversion journey mapping. Treat CRO as a post-signup problem only.

3. **SEO-adjacent guides (Ahrefs, Backlinko)**: Cover how to drive traffic, rarely how to convert it. No pillar content connecting B2B SEO strategy to conversion rates.

### Common Sections (must cover)
- What is CRO for SaaS (definition)
- SaaS conversion rate benchmarks (what's "good")
- Key conversion points in a SaaS funnel
- Landing page / sign-up page optimization
- Pricing page optimization
- Free trial CRO
- A/B testing for SaaS
- Tools used

### Content Gaps (differentiation opportunities)
1. **No SEO-to-conversion intent mapping**: All CRO guides assume paid traffic. Zero content connects organic search intent (informational → commercial → transactional) to conversion strategy. Users arriving from a "what is technical SEO" article should NOT see the same CTA as users arriving from "best SEO agency for SaaS."

2. **No self-serve vs. sales-assisted distinction**: B2B SaaS has two fundamentally different conversion paths — product-led growth (PLG, free trial → paid) and sales-led growth (SLG, demo request → contract). CRO tactics differ completely. No competitor makes this distinction cleanly.

3. **No buying committee framework**: Every B2C CRO tactic assumes one decision-maker. B2B SaaS buying committees have 5-7 stakeholders. CRO for a procurement buyer is different from CRO for the technical champion. No competitor covers this.

4. **No pipeline-yield framing**: Most CRO content measures micro-conversions (clicks, sign-ups) in isolation. No article calculates the end-to-end yield: organic visitor → MQL → SQL → trial → paid → expansion. This pipeline yield view is how SEO managers justify CRO investment to CFOs.

5. **No content-tier CTA strategy**: The right CTA depends on where in the buyer journey the content sits. Pillar/informational content should drive newsletter or resource download CTAs, not demo requests. Mid-funnel comparison content should drive demo CTAs. No competitor maps this out.

## 3. Differentiating Angle

**The core idea**: CRO for B2B SaaS is not one problem — it's four distinct conversion problems stacked on top of each other, each requiring a different strategy:

1. **Intent conversion** — Are you attracting the right visitors from organic search? (Traffic quality before tactics)
2. **Page conversion** — Are your landing, pricing, and trial pages converting interested visitors? (Classic CRO)
3. **Journey conversion** — Are your content CTAs matched to buyer stage? (Informational vs. commercial vs. transactional content)
4. **Pipeline conversion** — Are free trial or demo leads converting to paid? (Post-click, product + sales)

Most CRO guides only cover problem #2. This article covers all four — and shows how your SEO strategy directly determines the ceiling on #2, #3, and #4.

**The framing**: "Your conversion rate problem might actually be a traffic quality problem. Fix the content funnel architecture before you touch button colors."
