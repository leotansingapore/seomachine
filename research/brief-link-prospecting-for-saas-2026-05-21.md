## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Top Competitor Coverage Patterns

Most top-ranking articles on "how to find link building prospects" or "link prospecting" cover:
- Ahrefs Link Intersect / Broken Link Checker as the primary prospecting workflow
- Skyscraper technique (find top content, outreach to who links to it)
- Guest post prospecting via Google search operators ("write for us" + niche)
- Generic outreach CRM tools (Pitchbox, BuzzStream) without explanation of how to fill them
- One-size-fits-all guidance built around content sites and e-commerce -- not SaaS products

### Content Gaps (What Competitors Miss)

1. **SaaS passive link sources are completely ignored**: SaaS products earn links through integration marketplace listings, SaaS review sites (G2, Capterra, GetApp, Product Hunt), and partner/technology pages -- all of which require no ongoing cold outreach. No existing guide covers this as a structured prospecting category.

2. **Integration ecosystem as a prospecting goldmine**: Listing on Zapier, Make, HubSpot App Marketplace, Salesforce AppExchange, or Shopify App Store generates high-DA contextual links from exact-match integration pages. Competitors do not treat these as link prospects at all.

3. **SaaS-specific qualifier criteria**: Not all DR50 domains are equal for a SaaS product. A link from a SaaS newsletter (e.g., SaaS Weekly, Product Hunt newsletter) drives more qualified referral traffic than a generic content site with the same DR. Competitors give generic DA-based filters with no SaaS-specific context.

4. **Prospecting beyond cold outreach**: Cold outreach is one vector. The others -- community appearances, podcast guest spots, analyst relations, journalist source databases -- are prospecting activities that don't involve sending a cold email. No guide distinguishes these clearly.

5. **How to qualify a prospect before investing outreach time**: Most guides jump from "find prospects" to "send the email" without a qualification step. For SaaS teams running lean, qualifying upfront (traffic, relevance, editorial vs. sponsored, link type) prevents wasted outreach cycles.

6. **Building prospect lists at scale without scraping**: Ahrefs exports + Apollo + LinkedIn are the standard stack, but no guide walks through the workflow end-to-end in a way a junior SEO could replicate without a senior to shadow.

### Differentiation Strategy

The core reframe: link prospecting for SaaS is a two-track system. Track 1 is active prospecting (outreach-based: content sites, guest posts, digital PR targets). Track 2 is passive prospecting (product-led: directories, marketplaces, integration platforms, review sites). Most guides only cover Track 1. This article covers both, with SaaS-specific criteria for each. The angle "50+ qualified prospects per month" anchors the headline and body in a concrete, believable output metric that practitioners can benchmark against.

## 3. Recommended Article Outline

```
H1: Link prospecting for SaaS: how to find 50+ qualified prospects per month

Introduction (150-180 words)
- Hook: Most SaaS teams do link prospecting wrong -- they treat it like an e-commerce or media site problem. SaaS has a distinct prospect universe that generic guides miss entirely.
- Direct answer: Define the two-track prospecting model (active vs. passive)
- Key Takeaways block (5 bullets)

H2: Why SaaS link prospecting is different from generic link building
  - Buyer-journey relevance matters more than DA alone
  - SaaS has passive link sources that e-commerce doesn't
  - The integration and review ecosystem is unique to software products
  - Volume target: 50 qualified prospects/month is realistic; 500 is noise

H2: Track 1 -- Active prospecting (outreach-based)
  H3: Content site prospecting (Link Intersect + topic relevance filter)
    - Ahrefs Link Intersect workflow against 3 competitors
    - Filter: DR 30+, traffic 1K+/mo, editorial (not sponsored) links
  H3: Guest post prospecting (search operator method)
    - Operators: "write for us" + [SaaS vertical], "contributor guidelines" + [audience type]
    - Qualify: does the site have real traffic and editorial standards?
  H3: Resource page prospecting
    - Find curated lists and "best tools" pages in your vertical
    - Approach: offer to be added, not to replace a listed competitor
  H3: Podcast and community prospecting
    - SaaS-focused podcasts that feature guests (host earns DR from show notes links)
    - Community content (Hacker News "Show HN", Indie Hackers, Reddit AMAs) as contextual link sources

H2: Track 2 -- Passive prospecting (product-led)
  H3: SaaS review sites and directories
    - G2, Capterra, GetApp, Software Advice, SourceForge, AlternativeTo
    - Product Hunt -- launch page earns DA90+ link on day one; profile link is permanent
    - TrustRadius, Crozdesk, SaaSHub
    - Action: claim all listings, optimize profile pages (these are link assets, not just review pages)
  H3: Integration marketplace listings
    - Zapier, Make (Integromat), HubSpot App Marketplace, Salesforce AppExchange, Shopify App Store, Slack App Directory, Microsoft AppSource
    - Each listing is a DR 70-90 contextual link with your product name as anchor
    - Action: list every integration you have; build integrations specifically to earn these links
  H3: Partner and technology partner pages
    - Identify your key tech stack partners (CRM, payment processor, analytics, CDP)
    - Most partner program pages include a "works with" or "technology ecosystem" section with links
    - Action: join partner programs; request inclusion on partner pages

H2: How to qualify a prospect before outreach
  - The 4-filter system: (1) Relevance to SaaS buyer, (2) Editorial vs. sponsored, (3) Traffic signal (Ahrefs traffic > 500/mo), (4) Link type (dofollow vs. nofollow)
  - Relevance score: does this site's audience make purchasing decisions for SaaS products?
  - Red flags: link farms, PBNs, thin content, ad-heavy layouts
  - Green flags: bylined authors, real social following, genuine editorial links to similar SaaS tools

H2: The prospecting stack (tools and workflow)
  - Ahrefs (Link Intersect, Content Explorer, Site Explorer) -- primary prospecting tool
  - Hunter.io -- find decision-maker emails for outreach
  - Apollo.io -- firmographic data for larger outreach programs
  - Google Sheets -- prospect tracking (simpler than a CRM at <200 prospects/month)
  - Pitchbox / BuzzStream -- optional, for teams doing 200+ outreach emails/month
  - Workflow: export from Ahrefs → qualify in Sheets → enrich with Hunter → outreach

H2: Building to 50 qualified prospects per month
  - Monthly cadence: 2 hours of prospecting per week = 8 hours/month → 50+ qualified prospects
  - Week 1: Run Link Intersect for 3 competitors, qualify output
  - Week 2: Search operator guest post sweep, qualify + add to sheet
  - Week 3: Audit all passive sources -- any new directories to claim? Any new integration marketplaces?
  - Week 4: Community sweep (Reddit, Hacker News, Indie Hackers) for contextual opportunities
  - Tracking: prospect_date, source_type, domain, DR, traffic, relevance_score, outreach_status

H2: FAQ (4 questions)
  - How many prospects do I need to get 5 links per month?
  - Should I prospect manually or use a tool like Pitchbox?
  - What's the difference between a link prospect and a link target?
  - How do I find prospects when my SaaS is in a very niche industry?

Conclusion with dual CTA
  - Internal link back to parent pillar + anchor text optimization article
  - CTA 1: Download the prospect tracking template
  - CTA 2: Book a link audit
```
