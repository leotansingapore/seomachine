## Competitor Gap Analysis

**Competitors ranking for this keyword**: HubSpot, Content Marketing Institute, Semrush, Backlinko, Demand Gen Report, Salesforce Blog

### What every competitor does
- Describes B2B content marketing as "creating valuable content for your audience" without defining what audience means in a multi-stakeholder B2B context
- Presents a generic content funnel (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU) without mapping it to B2B's 84-day average sales cycle
- Lists content types (blog, video, whitepaper, case study) without explaining when each appears in the buyer journey or which stakeholder it addresses
- Measures success via traffic metrics — almost no competitor explains B2B-specific content attribution in pipeline terms
- Ignores the dark funnel entirely: the 70% of B2B research that happens before first vendor contact

### Five gaps no competitor fills

**Gap 1: No multi-stakeholder content architecture**  
B2B buying involves 5.4 stakeholders on average. The marketing director, CTO, CFO, end user, and procurement contact each search for different content at different stages. No existing B2B content strategy guide covers how to map content assets to each stakeholder — what they need, when they need it, and how to build it into a coherent architecture.

**Gap 2: No four-layer content framework for B2B buying cycles**  
The generic TOFU/MOFU/BOFU model is too crude for an 84-day B2B buying cycle. The actual B2B content need has four distinct layers: dark funnel awareness content, evaluation/category content, decision/comparison content, and sales enablement content. No competitor maps these four layers to actual B2B buyer behavior.

**Gap 3: No pipeline-first prioritization logic**  
Every guide recommends "starting with your audience" — none recommend starting with Layer 3 (comparison and decision content) because that's where deals are won or lost, not Layer 1 (awareness). The counterintuitive sequencing logic for a new B2B content strategy (build BOFU first, awareness last) is absent from every competitor guide.

**Gap 4: No B2B-specific content metrics**  
Competitors measure B2B content by traffic, shares, and email list growth. Zero competitors explain the four B2B-relevant metrics: pipeline-influenced content volume, qualified lead rate from organic, content consumption depth, and sales cycle assist rate. These are the metrics that connect content to revenue.

**Gap 5: No stakeholder × journey stage content matrix**  
A practical, worked example of what a full B2B content architecture looks like — every stakeholder, every stage, every content asset mapped in a table a practitioner can adapt — does not exist in any competing guide.

## Differentiating Angle

**Frame B2B content strategy as buyer journey architecture, not editorial planning.**

The core argument: B2B content strategy isn't about publishing a blog post each week and hoping it ranks. It's about architecting four layers of content that cover every buyer persona, at every stage of an 84-day buying cycle, before your competitor does. The output isn't an editorial calendar — it's a content system that generates pipeline.
