### Top 3 Competitor Articles (Based on Typical SERP for This Keyword)

**1. Ahrefs Blog** — "How to Create an SEO Content Strategy (That Actually Gets Results)"
- ~3,500 words
- Very tactical — focuses on keyword research → clustering → brief → write cycle

**2. Semrush Blog** — "Content Strategy for SEO: A Complete Guide"
- ~4,000 words
- Comprehensive but generic — covers everything shallowly
- No differentiation between content types and their intent

**3. Backlinko** — "SEO Content Strategy: The Definitive Guide"
- ~2,800 words
- Strong opening hook, good structure
- Heavy on Brian Dean's personal brand
- **Gap**: Outdated tactics (thin AI search section), no agency-client POV

### Common Sections (Must Cover)
All top 10 articles include:
1. What is an SEO content strategy (definition)
2. Keyword research as the foundation
3. Topic clusters / pillar page model
4. Content calendar / publishing cadence
5. On-page optimization
6. Measuring results

### Content Gaps (Our Opportunity)
- **No one covers ROI framing**: Competitors describe process but don't say "here's what a working content strategy is worth in revenue"
- **Agency POV missing**: All competitors write for DIYers — no one writes from "here's what we build for clients who pay $X/month"
- **AI search angle thin**: Most articles predate AI search optimization. Our GEO/AICO guidelines are a genuine differentiator.
- **Timeline realism**: Nobody says "Month 1 looks like X, Month 6 looks like Y." Readers don't know what to expect.
- **Prioritization**: Nobody tells you what to do first if you have limited time/budget.

### Differentiation Strategy
Lead with the **ROI/revenue angle** and the **agency systems perspective**. Our competitive moat: we've built content strategies that moved needle for clients, and we can show the actual system. Position this as "what a professional content strategy looks like" vs. "here's a checklist."
