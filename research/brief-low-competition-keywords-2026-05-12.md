## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Who ranks for "low competition keywords"
Ahrefs, Semrush, Backlinko, and Moz rank for the primary term. Consistent pattern: all are SEO tool vendors whose articles funnel toward their keyword explorer. The gap is that no vendor article explains how to verify competition independently (i.e., without relying on a single tool's KD score), and none connects low competition keyword targeting to the cluster authority strategy that makes finding these keywords scalable.

### Content Gaps (Our Opportunity)
1. **No SERP verification process**: Every guide says "find low KD keywords in [tool]." None explains how to manually audit the SERP to confirm the KD score is accurate -- which matters because tools frequently score keywords incorrectly.
2. **Domain authority relative framing missing**: Guides list KD thresholds (target under 30, under 20, etc.) without explaining that "low competition" is relative to YOUR domain authority. A KD 40 keyword is low competition for a DA 60 site.
3. **No cluster integration**: Finding a single low competition keyword in isolation is less valuable than finding a cluster of them. No competitor article explains how to map low competition variants to topic cluster architecture.
4. **Missing "false low" warning**: Some keywords appear low competition in tools but have high-authority sites ranking via topical authority (not backlinks). New sites lose time writing for these. A warning section here is a clear differentiator.

### Differentiation Strategy
Lead with the reframe: low competition doesn't mean low value -- it means underpriced. A keyword that a DA 60 site ignores because it's "too small" is exactly the keyword a new or growing site should target. The method sections go two layers deeper than competitors: find the candidates, then verify before committing writing resources.
