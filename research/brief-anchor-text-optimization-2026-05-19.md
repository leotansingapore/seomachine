## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Top Competitor Coverage Patterns

Most top-ranking articles on "anchor text optimization" cover:
- Taxonomy of anchor text types (exact match, partial match, branded, naked URL, generic, image)
- Penguin update history and over-optimization penalties
- Generic ratio recommendations (often quoted without source)
- Basic audit instructions using Ahrefs or Majestic screenshots
- One-size-fits-all guidance that doesn't differentiate by site type

### Content Gaps (What Competitors Miss)

1. **SaaS-specific anchor profiles**: No competitor explains how a SaaS product's brand name, product features, and integration pages naturally generate distinct anchor patterns -- and how to lean into that rather than fight it.
2. **AI search / entity signals**: Anchor text increasingly functions as an entity signal in AI-assisted search (Gemini, Perplexity, SearchGPT). Competitors discuss Penguin but ignore how LLMs use anchor context to understand what a page *is*.
3. **Practical audit workflow**: Most guides say "run an Ahrefs site:// report" and stop there. No article walks through the decision tree: what to do when you find over-optimized exact match anchors, how to request anchor changes from referring domains, or when to disavow vs. outreach-fix.
4. **Acquisition-phase vs. profile-management distinction**: There are two separate problems -- (a) how to set anchor text when you're actively acquiring links, and (b) how to manage/correct an existing profile. Competitors conflate them. This article should separate them clearly.
5. **Integration page anchor patterns**: For SaaS, pages like `/integrations/salesforce` earn anchors like "Salesforce integration" -- a form of partial-match keyword anchor that's naturally earned, not manufactured. No competitor covers this as a strategic opportunity.
6. **Internal anchor text is underserved**: Almost all anchor text guides focus exclusively on external (inbound) links. Internal anchor text strategy is equally important for page authority flow, and virtually no competitor covers both in one guide.

### Differentiation Strategy

Open with the framing that anchor text has two audiences: Google's ranking algorithm (well-covered by competitors) and AI search engines (ignored by competitors). Most guides treat anchor text as a penalty-avoidance exercise. This article reframes it as an active trust signal -- both for traditional search and for the AI systems increasingly surfacing B2B SaaS content. The SaaS-specific context (branded anchors, integration pages, product-feature anchors) creates a natural differentiation from the generic SEO content that dominates this SERP.
