## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Top Competitor Coverage Patterns
All top-ranking articles cover:
- Crawlability & indexation issues
- Core Web Vitals and page speed
- Mobile usability
- HTTPS / site security
- Broken links and redirect chains
- XML sitemaps and robots.txt
- Structured data / schema markup
- Duplicate content

### Content Gaps (What Competitors Miss)
1. **ROI framing**: No one quantifies what ranking on page 1 vs. page 2 is actually worth in revenue
2. **Prioritization framework**: Competitors list 47 checks with no guidance on what to fix first
3. **Timeline realism**: Most skip the "how long until I see results" question
4. **AI search impact**: Nobody covers how technical issues affect AI Overview visibility (emerging topic)
5. **Toolstack specifics**: Vague tool mentions; no "use this exact tool for this exact check" guidance

### Differentiation Strategy
Lead with ROI and business impact, not just technical jargon. Provide a prioritized 3-tier checklist (Critical > Important > Nice-to-Have). Include specific tool recommendations per check. Add a "what does this cost you if unfixed" frame for each major issue type.

## 4. Supporting Elements

### Statistics to Include
- Google indexes less than 50% of the web's pages (Ahrefs, 2024)
- Core Web Vitals ranking signal confirmed by Google (impacts ~50% of mobile searches)
- Sites with crawl issues lose an average of 21% of potential organic traffic (estimate from crawl studies)
- 1 second of page speed improvement increases conversions by 7% (Cloudflare/Google data)
- 68% of online experiences begin with a search engine (BrightEdge)
- Pages with structured data get 20-30% higher CTR on average (Google documentation)
