## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Why vendors dominate this SERP
Ahrefs, Semrush, and Moz rank for "long-tail keywords" because they use the concept to sell their keyword tools. Their articles explain the concept but are oriented toward tool demos. The gap: nobody connects long-tail keyword targeting to topic cluster strategy and measures outcomes at the cluster level.

### Content Gaps (Our Opportunity)
1. **No cluster-level strategy**: Every guide tells you to find long-tail keywords. None explains how they compound as a cluster strategy -- how supporting articles target long-tail variants that lift pillar authority on head terms.
2. **Missing conversion data framing**: Most guides note that long-tail converts better but don't give readers a practical framework for prioritizing long-tail by intent stage.
3. **No "when long-tail is the wrong choice" coverage**: Gaps in practical guidance about when a supposedly "long-tail" keyword is actually just a niche head term (high difficulty, low volume).
4. **New site context underserved**: The best guides for long-tail are written for mid-authority sites. New sites need a clearer playbook (DA 0-20: target KD 0-25, avoid anything competitive).

### Differentiation Strategy
Lead with the reframe: long-tail keywords aren't the consolation prize you target before going after real keywords. For 90% of businesses, they ARE the real keywords -- higher conversion, faster ranking, and a compounding cluster effect that eventually lifts your head terms. Show the data, tell the mini-story, give the 5-step process.

## 4. Supporting Elements

### Statistics to Include
1. ~70% of all Google searches are long-tail keywords (Ahrefs) -- most have 3+ words
2. Long-tail keywords convert at 2.5x the rate of head terms (WordStream industry data -- note as "industry benchmark")
3. 96.55% of web pages get zero organic traffic (Ahrefs) -- long-tail targeting is the primary lever to escape that statistic
4. Sites that publish topic cluster content (pillar + supporting articles targeting long-tail variants) see 3x the indexed pages reach page 1 vs. standalone publishing (HubSpot)

### Mini-Stories
