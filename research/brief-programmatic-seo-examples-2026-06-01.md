## Competitor Analysis

### Current SERP landscape
Every top-ranking article uses the same three or four brands: Zapier, Airbnb, TripAdvisor, Canva. They name-drop those companies and move on. None of them:
1. Explain WHY those examples work at a granular level (data structure, quality decisions)
2. Show what programmatic SEO looks like at B2B SaaS scale (40–100 integrations, not 7 million)
3. Show what bad programmatic SEO looks like — the failure patterns that get penalised
4. Give readers a framework to evaluate whether an example is worth copying for their situation

### Key gaps
1. **Anatomy-level analysis**: competitors tell you what Zapier does, not why each page passes the quality bar
2. **Realistic B2B examples**: nobody covers Pipedrive's marketplace, Intercom's integration pages, or Notion's template library — all achievable without an engineering army
3. **Failure patterns**: no competitor documents what city-swap boilerplate looks like or why thin AI-generated definitions get penalised
4. **Transferable diagnostic**: readers want to evaluate examples, not just admire them

---

## Differentiation Strategy

Lead with a 4-question diagnostic framework for evaluating programmatic SEO examples before presenting examples. This is the angle no competitor uses — it primes readers to think analytically rather than just copy the biggest brands.

Structure:
1. The 4-question diagnostic (upfront, before examples)
2. Great examples by model — with anatomy, not just names
3. What bad examples look like (failure patterns)
4. How to apply the diagnostic to your own site
