## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Top Competitor Coverage Patterns

Most top-ranking articles on "digital PR for B2B SaaS" or "SaaS PR strategy" cover:
- General digital PR definitions (newsjacking, data-led content, reactive PR) with consumer brand examples
- Agency-pitched frameworks that require a dedicated PR team to execute
- Generic press release guidance ("write a press release, distribute via PR Newswire") that earns no editorial links
- Link building framed as "send your article to journalists" without explaining how to create an asset journalists actually care about
- Coverage aimed at Series B+ companies with a marketing function, not early-stage SaaS teams

### Content Gaps (What Competitors Miss)

1. **The B2B SaaS media landscape is completely unaddressed**: Generic digital PR guides don't name the specific publications, newsletters, and podcasts that cover B2B SaaS. Trade press (G2 Reach, SaaStr, Product Hunt, Software Advice Blog), SaaS-focused newsletters (The SaaS Weekly, Lenny's Newsletter, Product Hunt Daily), and analyst relations (for seed/Series A) are absent from every competitor guide. A B2B SaaS founder reading a generic digital PR guide has no idea where to point their pitch.

2. **Original research methodology for SaaS products is missing**: Every guide says "create original data" but none explain how a SaaS company with a small user base actually does it: aggregating usage data, running an industry survey with 50-100 respondents, analysing public data from G2 or ProductHunt reviews. For a company without millions of data points, those alternatives are how you create a linkable data asset.

3. **No guide addresses the lean budget constraint directly**: Most digital PR content assumes access to a PR agency or at minimum a dedicated PR manager. The gap is the "minimum viable digital PR operation" -- what a 2-person marketing team can run in 4-6 hours per month without a PR retainer.

4. **Founder thought leadership as a link acquisition play is underdeveloped**: For early-stage SaaS, founder credibility is often the strongest PR asset. Positioning the founder as a practitioner-expert (via contributed articles, podcast appearances, LinkedIn commentary picked up by journalists) generates editorial links in a way product-led PR cannot. No competitor covers this as a structured link acquisition tactic.

5. **Digital PR ROI measurement for SaaS is absent**: Generic guides conflate brand mentions with editorial links and don't distinguish between traffic-driving coverage and link-equity coverage. SaaS teams need to measure: editorial dofollow links earned, referral traffic from coverage, share-of-voice in target publications, and whether coverage translates to trial signups.

6. **The distinction between PR and link building is never made clear**: Many SaaS teams don't know that a press mention without a link is not the same as a link building outcome. The brief should clarify: digital PR for SEO requires link attribution, not just brand mention coverage.

### Differentiation Strategy

The core reframe: digital PR for B2B SaaS is a 3-play system, not a PR retainer. Play 1 is original research (data-led assets that journalists cite). Play 2 is milestone newsjacking (funding, product launches, customer milestones pitched to relevant outlets). Play 3 is founder thought leadership (the founder as a quoted source and contributor). A 2-person marketing team can rotate through these three plays across a quarter and generate 8-12 editorial links without an agency. The angle "without a PR agency" anchors the headline and differentiates from agency-pitched competitor content.

## 3. Recommended Article Outline

```
H1: Digital PR for B2B SaaS: how to earn editorial links without a PR agency

Introduction (150-180 words)
- Hook: Most digital PR advice is written for consumer brands or companies with a PR retainer. B2B SaaS has a different media landscape, different story assets, and a different buyer for your story.
- Direct answer: Define digital PR in the SaaS context (editorial links via media coverage vs. traditional PR brand awareness)
- Key Takeaways block (5 bullets)

H2: What digital PR means for a B2B SaaS company (and what it doesn't)
  - Digital PR for SEO = editorial links from journalists and newsletter writers, not brand mentions
  - Press releases distributed via PR Newswire don't earn editorial links
  - What counts: original data cited by journalists, product milestone coverage, founder quotes in round-up articles
  - What doesn't count for link building: unlinked brand mentions, social mentions, podcast appearances without show note links

H2: The 3 digital PR plays that work for early-stage SaaS
  H3: Play 1 -- Original research and data reports
    - Why data earns links: journalists need citable sources; SaaS products are a natural source of usage data
    - How to create a data asset without millions of data points:
      * Option A: Aggregate your own usage data (anonymised) into a benchmark report
      * Option B: Run an industry survey (50-100 respondents via LinkedIn, existing customers, Typeform)
      * Option C: Analyse public data (G2 reviews, Product Hunt upvotes, GitHub stars, public SaaS pricing changes)
    - Format: "State of [Your Category] Report" or "[Year] [Vertical] Benchmark"
    - Distribution: send to 10-15 targeted journalists/newsletter writers before publication; embargo for 24-48 hours
  H3: Play 2 -- Product milestone announcements
    - Coverage-worthy milestones: funding rounds (even pre-seed is a story for vertical press), new integrations with name-brand platforms, customer milestone (100th customer, first enterprise contract), notable feature launch
    - Who covers SaaS milestones: TechCrunch Startups, SaaStr blog, Hacker News, The SaaS CTO, G2 News
    - Pitch format: 3-sentence email with the milestone, the "why now" hook, and a link to more detail
    - Link outcome: coverage typically includes a link to your product page or announcement post
  H3: Play 3 -- Founder thought leadership
    - The founder as a quoted source: journalists writing about your category want practitioner quotes, not vendor quotes
    - Journalist source databases: HARO (Help a Reporter Out), Qwoted, SourceBottle -- respond to journalist queries for B2B SaaS topics
    - Contributed articles: pitch a bylined piece to one trade publication per month; include a bio link
    - LinkedIn as a feeder: journalists monitor LinkedIn for practitioner takes; a thoughtful post on a trending SaaS topic gets DM'd by journalists within 24-48 hours
    - Link outcome: byline links from trade press, quotes with source attribution links

H2: The B2B SaaS media landscape: where your coverage lives
  - Trade and vertical publications: TechCrunch, VentureBeat, The Information (for enterprise SaaS), SaaStr, G2 Reach Blog, Software Advice Blog, Capterra News
  - SaaS-focused newsletters (high link equity, small but targeted audiences): Lenny's Newsletter, The SaaS Playbook, Product Hunt Daily, SaaS Weekly, Demand Curve
  - Analyst relations at scale: Gartner and Forrester require a PR budget; but G2 Grid Reports, Capterra Shortlist, and GetApp Category Leaders are analyst-lite and accessible at any stage
  - Podcasts with editorial authority: show notes links from SaaS podcasts (Acquired, Lenny's Podcast, The SaaS Podcast, Founders) are permanent, contextual links; target shows where the host links to every guest

H2: How to pitch: what actually gets a reply from a B2B tech journalist
  - Length: 3-5 sentences max. Journalists delete long pitches on first glance.
  - What to lead with: the data point or milestone, not your company description
  - Subject line formula: [Data hook] -- exclusive for [Publication name]
  - Exclusivity and embargo: offer 24-hour exclusivity; it increases reply rate and often guarantees a link
  - Follow-up: one follow-up after 48 hours. No more.
  - Who to pitch: find the journalist covering your beat on each publication (check their recent bylines, not the editor)

H2: Measuring digital PR ROI
  - Primary metric: editorial dofollow links earned (track in Ahrefs > new links, filter by "editorial" context)
  - Secondary metrics: referral traffic from coverage (GA4 > acquisition > referral source), share-of-voice in target publications (track manually via Google Alerts + Ahrefs Brand Monitoring)
  - Conversion metric: do trials/signups spike during or after coverage? UTM-tag all press mentions you control
  - Reporting cadence: review link outcomes monthly, share-of-voice quarterly

H2: The minimum viable digital PR operation
  - 4-6 hours per month total
  - Month structure: 1 data asset or milestone pitch per quarter (5 hours to create + 1 hour to distribute); respond to 3-5 HARO/Qwoted queries per month (30 mins/response); 1 contributed article pitch per month (30 mins to pitch, 2-3 hours to write when accepted)
  - Toolstack: Google Alerts (brand monitoring, free), HARO/Qwoted (journalist query monitoring, free), Ahrefs (link tracking), a media list in Google Sheets

H2: FAQ (4 questions)
  - Do I need a PR agency to run digital PR for my SaaS?
  - What's the difference between digital PR and traditional PR for link building?
  - How long does it take to earn editorial links from digital PR?
  - My SaaS is in a niche vertical -- which publications should I target?

Conclusion with dual CTA
  - Internal link back to parent pillar + link prospecting article
  - CTA 1: Download the digital PR pitch template
  - CTA 2: Book a link audit / digital PR strategy call
```
