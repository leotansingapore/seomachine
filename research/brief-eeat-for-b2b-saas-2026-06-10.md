## Competitor Gap Analysis

**Competitors ranking for E-E-A-T keywords**: Search Engine Journal, Semrush blog, Moz, Backlinko, Neil Patel, Content Marketing Institute

### What every competitor does
- Explain E-E-A-T in generic terms (definitions + checklist)
- Focus almost exclusively on YMYL niches: health, finance, legal — "show a doctor's credentials"
- Recommend surface fixes: add author bios, get more backlinks, update content regularly
- No B2B-specific framing anywhere
- No distinction between how E-E-A-T applies differently for SaaS products vs. editorial publishers

### Five gaps no competitor addresses

**Gap 1: No B2B SaaS-specific E-E-A-T framing**  
Every existing E-E-A-T guide treats it as a YMYL problem — health, legal, financial advice. B2B SaaS is never mentioned. But E-E-A-T applies directly to software buying decisions: when a VP of Engineering evaluates DevOps tooling, they're asking the same trust questions a patient asks when reading medical content. The trust stake is different in nature (commercial, not health), but the E-E-A-T signals required to answer it are equally demanding. No guide addresses this.

**Gap 2: No "Experience" layer operationalized for SaaS**  
The first "E" (Experience) was added in 2022 and most guides still focus on Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. For B2B SaaS, "experience" has a specific meaning: have you actually implemented what you're writing about? Have your customers solved the problem you're describing? No guide covers how to operationalize practitioner experience in B2B content — original data programs, case study integration standards, SME interview protocols.

**Gap 3: No company-level authority stack for SaaS**  
Existing guides treat authoritativeness as a backlink problem. For B2B SaaS, company authority is built through a stack: analyst relations (Gartner/Forrester/G2), integration partner pages, trade press coverage, original research, customer advocacy. These are relationship-gated and require 6-18 months to build. No guide maps this B2B-specific authority stack.

**Gap 4: No connection between E-E-A-T and commercial trust signals**  
In B2B SaaS, trustworthiness isn't just about citing sources and displaying author credentials — it's about commercial credibility: SOC 2 compliance visibility, transparent pricing, named customer references, G2 review volume. No existing guide connects the E-E-A-T trustworthiness dimension to the commercial trust signals that actually move B2B buyers.

**Gap 5: No E-E-A-T audit process for SaaS content**  
Guides recommend "improving" E-E-A-T but provide no systematic audit methodology. For a B2B SaaS company with 100+ blog posts, knowing which articles to upgrade first (by traffic, ranking position, content gap) requires a prioritized process. No guide provides one.

## Differentiating Angle

**Frame E-E-A-T as the trust infrastructure that powers B2B buying decisions — not a YMYL compliance checkbox.**

The core argument: In B2B SaaS, every piece of content is implicitly trying to answer the buyer's trust question — "is this company safe to bet my reputation on?" E-E-A-T is the mechanism by which your content infrastructure answers yes. It has four layers specific to B2B SaaS (Experience = practitioner depth; Expertise = author + topical authority; Authoritativeness = analyst relations + partner ecosystem + original research; Trustworthiness = technical + commercial trust signals), and upgrading any layer has compound effects across the others.

## Section Outline

### Section 1: Why E-E-A-T Matters in B2B SaaS — Differently Than You Think
- The YMYL misconception: why B2B teams assume E-E-A-T doesn't apply
- The actual B2B trust stack: buyer evaluates author → company → technical depth → commercial legitimacy
- Where B2B SaaS content fails E-E-A-T: thin content written by non-practitioners, no named authors, no original data
- What Google's Quality Rater Guidelines say about technical/software content (expertise in specialized domains applies)

### Section 2: Experience — The Practitioner Layer
- What "experience" means for B2B SaaS: you or your customers have done the thing
- Original customer data program: quarterly benchmarks, platform analytics, survey studies
- Case study integration standards: specific outcomes (not "grew revenue"), named companies, timeline
- SME interview protocol as standard editorial input
- Real implementation screenshots vs. stock photos

### Section 3: Expertise — Author and Topical Authority
- The byline problem: most SaaS content has no named author or a generic junior writer
- Building author entities: bio pages with structured data, LinkedIn presence, external publications
- Author schema markup (Person schema with credentials and article history)
- The SME author model: generalist writers + practitioner contributors + editorial review
- Topical authority as a company-level expertise signal: pillar-and-cluster coverage depth
- Topic cluster architecture as an E-E-A-T signal (comprehensive subject coverage = expertise)
- Reference to B2B content strategy and SEO content strategy for implementation

### Section 4: Authoritativeness — Third-Party Validation
- The authority stack for B2B SaaS (not just backlinks):
  - Integration partner pages (AppExchange, Marketplace, partner portals)
  - Analyst relations: G2 Grid position, Gartner/Forrester coverage, category report inclusion
  - Trade press editorial coverage (not press releases — earned bylines and citations)
  - Original research as authority multiplier (State of X reports become link magnets)
  - Speaking and events (conference pages generate high-authority citation links)
  - Customer advocacy (case studies, tools-we-use posts on customer blogs)
- Link to link building for B2B SaaS and digital PR for B2B SaaS for tactics

### Section 5: Trustworthiness — Technical and Commercial Trust Architecture
- Technical trust signals:
  - HTTPS, security headers
  - Compliance badges (SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, HIPAA) — displayed, linked to docs
  - Uptime transparency and SLA visibility
  - Privacy policy written for humans
  - Core Web Vitals (slow sites erode enterprise trust)
  - Link to technical SEO audit for implementation
- Commercial trust signals:
  - Named customer references (logos, names — not "500+ companies")
  - Transparent pricing or clear custom-pricing explanation
  - G2/Capterra/Trustpilot review integration on relevant pages
  - Real About page: team photos, bios, company history
  - Terms of service orientation: customer-protective, not company-protective
- Content trust signals:
  - Last-updated dates displayed and maintained
  - External citations to authoritative sources for factual claims
  - No unsubstantiated superlatives ("industry-leading," "best-in-class")

### Section 6: E-E-A-T Content Audit — Where to Start
- Why auditing before fixing prevents wasted effort
- 7-step E-E-A-T audit process:
  1. Export organic traffic by URL from GA4
  2. Identify top 30 pages by organic sessions
  3. Author entity check: named author? bio page? schema markup?
  4. Experience check: original data or case studies, or only third-party citations?
  5. Trust check: external citations for factual claims? last-updated date displayed?
  6. Schema check: Article, Author, FAQ schema present?
  7. Competitive check: do competitors ranking above you have stronger author entities or original data?
- Prioritization matrix: position 5-15 pages + high traffic = highest E-E-A-T upgrade leverage
- Quick wins vs. long-term builds: bio pages and schema markup are immediate; original research program takes 6 months

### Section 7: Measuring E-E-A-T Progress
- E-E-A-T is an inference, not a score — measure the signals, not the metric
- Tracking table with proxy metrics:
  - Author entity: Google Knowledge Panel appearance
  - Branded search growth: GSC branded query volume trend
  - Backlink quality: average DA of linking domains (Ahrefs)
  - Review volume: G2/Capterra review count and recency
  - Content depth: schema completeness, word count per category
  - Ranking: position trend for target keywords
- Timeline: expect 3-6 months for E-E-A-T improvements to manifest in rankings
- Link to SEO reporting guide for tracking setup
