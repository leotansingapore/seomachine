## Five competitor gaps

1. **No account-based link building framework** — Existing guides treat link prospecting as finding any relevant, high-DA site. No guide applies ABM logic: identify the 40-60 publications your specific ICP accounts actually read, rank them by pipeline proximity, and build relationships with their editors over months rather than spraying outreach.

2. **No pipeline attribution model for links** — Guides measure B2B link building success with DA improvement or total backlink count. No guide explains how to attribute links to pipeline: surveying closed accounts to identify which publications influenced their decision, then reverse-engineering which of your existing links appear in that journey.

3. **No analyst relations as structured SEO channel** — Gartner, Forrester, IDC, G2, Capterra mentions are treated as PR wins, not engineered backlink opportunities. No guide explains how to structure analyst briefings, category submissions, and review cultivation as a systematic link-building program that places you in the exact sources enterprise buyers trust most.

4. **No partner co-marketing link architecture** — Integration partner pages, joint whitepapers, co-authored research, and partner ecosystem pages are the highest editorial-trust link sources in B2B. No guide treats partner co-marketing as a structured link acquisition program with a repeatable pipeline.

5. **No customer advocacy link program** — Links from customer company blogs, case study profiles, and "tools we use" pages are uniquely high-value in B2B: the linking site is your ICP, which sends both Google and human signals that you're trusted by the exact profile you're targeting. No guide addresses this as a standalone link building channel with a cultivation process.

## Differentiating angle

B2B link building fails when you apply mass-outreach logic to a relationship-driven buying process.

The highest-value links in B2B come from three sources: analyst firms your buyers consult, integration partners your buyers already use, and customers your prospects can call for references. None of these are won through outreach volume. All of them require structured relationship programs that operate more like account-based marketing than traditional link building.

The strategic shift: stop optimizing for link count and DA. Start optimizing for link placement in the buyer's research journey. A single mention in a Forrester Wave, a G2 category guide, or a Salesforce AppExchange integration page is worth more than 200 guest post links — because your buyer will actually see it at the moment they're forming a vendor shortlist.
